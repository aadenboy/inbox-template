    what is this???

    welcome to my inbox! to summarize, this is just a wrapper around a Google Forms document. you can send whatever you want through to me via this, and it also includes a canvas if you want to draw something!

    all of the fields in this form are completely optional, and you only need to fill one in order to submit the form (you can't send just your identity though...)

    note that everything that is sent is seen by me, and I may choose to post it online if I respond to it. I will obviously keep anything confidential hidden but general advisory is to Not Do That

    if you need a more urgent or serious inquiry, I implore you to please contact me via a more direct or secure method, such as via private messages, an email, or on my talk page on any of the wikis I am on. this form is purely for silly goodness! a last resort at best

    also take note that I will ignore any and all "joke responses", such as the jokers who sent in scribbles. you will not get your fun

    since this is a google forms wrapper, you have the option of filling in the form directly in case this site proves to be unreliable (found at the bottom of the website)

    general canvas help

    the canvas is a 250x200 pixel canvas for more free expressionism! the canvas supports both mouse (by extension drawing pad) and touch input. the canvas is saved to the form when you submit it, and you can also save it as a PNG image if you want to keep it for yourself

    the slider at the top controls the size of your brush strokes, and the input next to that controls the color of your brush strokes. the rightmost arrow buttons allow undoing and redoing your strokes, which can also be done with Ctrl/Cmd+Z and Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+Z respectively

    the tools to the left of the canvas determine what happens when you draw on the canvas. by default, this is set to the Pen tool (P). use the Line (L), Rectangle (R), and Oval (O) tools to inscribe their respective shapes, and hold Shift while drawing them to make a perfect shape. the Erase (E) and Fill (F) toggles are applied onto your stroke and combinable, erasing and/or filling your strokes respectively

    the list at the right controls the layers in your drawing, of which you may have up to 256 of. layers are drawing in descending order, with the first layer being the topmost layer. click "Add layer" to create a new layer, which automatically selects it. clicking "remove" removes the layer and all of its strokes. pressing the Move Up or Move Down buttons changes the ordering of the layer. the overall opacity of the layer can be modified with the input box, and the blending mode of the layer with those under it is selectable in the dropdown menu

    canvas import/export help

    in order to allow for google forms to store your drawing, your image is encoded as a Base64 string that I receive on my end. this is what the "size" corresponds to, and why extremely detailed images are unable to be sent in to me

    for your needs, you likely won't ever use these buttons; the "save png" button (which saves your drawing as a PNG image) will be all that you need

    but, if you're curious: pressing "export raw" will save the string to your clipboard, which can then be imported back by using the "import raw" button (assuming the string is in your clipboard)

    for developers:

    drawings are stored in a custom byte-encoded format. newer strings follow the format [version][attributes]:[data], where version is a number, attributes is a string of lowercase letters (denoting the properties of the data), and data is the base64 string. if the string does not match this format, it is assumed to be version 1 with no attributes. the base64 string can optinally be URL safe, but it is not required

    • version 1 (default): simply the raw byte data, and is the standard for future versions regarding stroke data. strokes follow this format:
      [r][g][b][size][length size][...length][...points]
      r, g, and b are the red, green, and blue values of the stroke's color, stored as bytes. size is the width of the stroke, stored as a byte. length size determines how many bytes the length of the stroke takes up, and that many bytes afterwards (...length) determine the amount of points in the stroke in little endian. the rest of the bytes (...points) are the X and Y positions of each point in the stroke, stored as pairs of bytes
    • version 2: the raw byte data is compressed using zlib, with the format being the same as version 1
    • version 3: in addition to versions 1 and 2...
      • the RGB values for all tools are followed by an additional byte for the alpha channel, and each tool is distinguished by a prefixed number. each prefix is also immediately followed by a digit determining if it is erasing (+0x01) and/or filling (+0x02)
      • strokes are preceded by 0x00 to signify that it is a stroke. the points in the stroke are also stored as a delta from the previous point (two's complement), with the exception of the first point. to disable this, append a to the attribute string.
      • the line, rectangle, and oval tools are now supported, being prefixed with 0x01, 0x02, and 0x03 respectively. the format for these tools is
        [tool][r][g][b][a][x1][y1][x2][y2]
        where tool is the tool's prefix, r, g, b, and a are the red, green, blue, and alpha values of the tool's color, and x1, y1, x2, and y2 are the coordinates of the tool's start and end points
      • layers reserve the code 0xff, with such data:
        [opacity][blending mode][visibility]
        where opacity is the oppacity of the layer, blending mode is the blending mode of the layer as an index to the ordering of the dropdown menu, and visibility is either 0x00 or 0x01 determining the visibility of the layer. all the strokes which follow are drawn on this layer until another layer is defined
